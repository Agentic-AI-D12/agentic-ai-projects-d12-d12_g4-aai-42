# Medical Guidelines for Common Diseases

## Kidney Stones (Renal Calculi)
- **Definition**: A small, hard deposit that forms in the kidneys and is often painful when passed.
- **Key Treatment Steps**:
  1. Increase fluid intake to at least 2-3 liters per day to flush the stones.
  2. Use pain relievers like ibuprofen or naproxen for discomfort.
  3. Medical therapy (Alpha-blockers) to help the stone pass more easily.
  4. Surgical options (Lithotripsy or Ureteroscopy) if the stone is too large.
- **Estimated Costs in India**:
  - Conservative treatment: ₹5,000 - ₹15,000
  - Laser Lithotripsy: ₹60,000 - ₹1,20,000
  - Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy (PCNL): ₹80,000 - ₹1,50,000

## Diabetes (Type 2)
- **Definition**: A chronic condition that affects the way the body processes blood sugar (glucose).
- **Key Treatment Steps**:
  1. Regular blood sugar monitoring.
  2. Healthy eating and weight management.
  3. Regular exercise (30 mins a day).
  4. Oral medications (Metformin) or Insulin therapy if needed.
- **Estimated Costs in India**:
  - Monthly medication & monitoring: ₹2,000 - ₹5,000
  - Annual comprehensive checkup: ₹10,000 - ₹20,000

## Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)
- **Definition**: A condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is too high.
- **Key Treatment Steps**:
  1. Reduce salt intake to less than 5g per day.
  2. DASH diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension).
  3. Regular physical activity.
  4. Antihypertensive medications as prescribed by a doctor.
- **Estimated Costs in India**:
  - Monthly medication: ₹500 - ₹2,000
  - Regular GP consultation: ₹500 - ₹1,000 per visit.
